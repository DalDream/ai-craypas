# Concept Examples

This document collects early examples of the AI Craypas interaction.

These examples are exploratory drafts.

They should not be treated as fully fact-checked pathways until each factual claim has been verified.

---

## 1. Fingernail → Magellan

### Core Question

How can a small biological object connect to a historical explorer?

### Possible Direction

    Fingernail
    → growth rate
    → gradual motion
    → tectonic plate movement
    → Pacific Plate
    → Strait of Magellan
    → Magellan

### Why It Is Interesting

The pathway attempts to connect biological growth and geological motion through shared scales of gradual change.

The intended discovery is not that fingernails and tectonic plates are literally the same, but that very small and very large processes can be compared through measurable movement over time.

### Intermediate Discoveries

Possible discoveries along this pathway include:

- keratinization as the biological process involved in fingernail formation
- the approximate growth rate of human fingernails
- the scale of tectonic plate movement
- the relationship between continental drift and modern plate tectonics
- the geography of the Strait of Magellan

The comparison between fingernail growth and plate movement must be verified carefully before being presented as a factual discovery.

### Validation Needed

- Verify the growth-rate comparison.
- Verify the geographic connection between plate movement and the Strait of Magellan.
- Clearly distinguish factual relationships from interpretive comparisons.

## 2. Alleyway → Glacier

### Path A: Ecology and Adaptation

This route explores how narrow urban spaces and glacier environments may be connected through ecological adaptation, shelter, microclimates, or survival strategies.

### Path B: Matter and Flow

This route explores how alleyways and glaciers may be connected through movement, pressure, channels, erosion, drainage, or material flow.

### Why Multiple Paths Matter

The same input pair can lead to different domains.

This is one of the central interaction hypotheses of AI Craypas:

> The same two concepts may contain more than one understandable route between them.

## 3. Orchid → Voyager

### Possible Direction

    Orchid
    → pollination signals
    → biological communication
    → signal transmission
    → long-distance communication
    → Deep Space Network
    → Voyager

### Why It Is Interesting

The pathway explores how biological signaling and deep-space communication may share the problem of transmitting a precise signal across distance.

### Intermediate Discoveries

Possible discoveries along this pathway include:

- the signaling strategies orchids use in pollination
- biological communication as a broader concept
- the problem of transmitting signals across long distances
- the role of the Deep Space Network in communicating with Voyager

The similarity between biological and engineered communication should be labeled as structural analogy rather than direct equivalence.

### Validation Needed

- Separate biological facts from structural analogy.
- Avoid overstating similarities between plant signaling and engineered communication systems.

## 4. Frieren → Post-it

### Possible Direction

    Frieren
    → accumulated knowledge
    → fragmented memory
    → indexing
    → external memory aids
    → pressure-sensitive adhesive
    → Post-it

### Why It Is Interesting

This pathway explores the relationship between long-lived memory, information organization, and lightweight tools for externalizing knowledge.

### Intermediate Discoveries

Possible discoveries along this pathway include:

- an unfamiliar term or concept
- a fact the user may not have known
- a new field or domain entered through an intermediate node
- a word worth saving and reusing in a later concept pair

These discoveries should come from the actual pathway rather than being unrelated bonus trivia.

### Validation Needed

- Clearly mark media references as cultural connections.
- Avoid presenting interpretive readings as objective facts.

---

## Example Design Rule

Every future example should include:

1. Start concept
2. End concept
3. Pathway nodes
4. Connection type for each hop
5. One-line explanation per hop
6. Intermediate discoveries
7. Discovery insight for the complete pathway
8. Saveable or reusable words
9. Fact-checking notes

A strong example should not only connect the endpoints.

It should contain at least one intermediate word, fact, domain, or perspective that feels worth remembering, saving, or exploring further.
